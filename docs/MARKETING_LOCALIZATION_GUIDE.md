# OASIS Marketing Localization Guide

This guide defines how the marketing site should adapt its voice across English, Polish, and German without drifting away from actual product truth.

## Purpose

OASIS does not sell one generic "property management app" story in every market.

The product already has a shared operational core:

- Command Center
- maintenance coordination
- tenant portal
- document workflow
- portfolio health
- security / auditability

What changes by locale is the framing.

## Locale personas

### English

Positioning:

- challenger
- decisive
- anti-noise
- action-oriented

What to emphasize:

- reclaim control
- stop fragmented workflows
- know the next move fast

Preferred language:

- "See OASIS in action"
- "What needs action"
- "Keep the workflow moving"

Avoid:

- flat enterprise jargon
- vague "all-in-one" claims with no operational specificity

### Polish

Positioning:

- pragmatic
- efficient
- modern, but not theatrical
- grounded in operational clarity

What to emphasize:

- oszczędność czasu
- porządek operacyjny
- sprawniejsze decyzje
- mniej chaosu informacyjnego

Preferred language:

- `briefing operacyjny`
- `pełna kontrola`
- `następny krok`
- `kolejki działań`

Avoid:

- literal translation of English metaphors
- overpromising "AI magic"
- slangy or needlessly provocative copy

Notes:

- When AI is mentioned, make the data/decision path feel concrete.
- Polish users are more likely to reward clarity than brand theatrics.

### German

Positioning:

- formal
- reliable
- structured
- explainable

What to emphasize:

- nachvollziehbare Entscheidungen
- operative Klarheit
- strukturierte Nachverfolgung
- belastbare Abläufe

Preferred language:

- `operative Kurzanalyse`
- `Nachvollziehbare Aufgabenpriorisierung`
- `Risiko-Analyse`
- `Handlungsbedarf`

Avoid:

- informal tone
- heavy metaphor
- loose startup phrasing
- unexplained AI claims

Notes:

- Use formal `Sie`.
- Treat legal and trust surfaces as product requirements, not footer decoration.
- Where possible, prefer "explainable" framing over "clever" framing.

## Shared content rules

1. Do not claim features that do not exist.
2. Keep AI copy tied to explainability, prioritization, or recommendation.
3. Keep screenshots and labels aligned to real product surfaces.
4. Prefer short, scan-friendly headers.
5. Translate intent, not just words.

## Legal / trust rules

### Germany

- `Impressum` must be visible from the footer.
- Public company/operator details should be completed before broader Germany-targeted paid or public campaign rollout.
- Formality and traceability matter more than "disruptor" energy.

### Poland

- Trust comes more from clarity and usefulness than from heavy legal framing.
- AI wording should suggest practical support, not black-box automation.

### English markets

- More room for challenger copy is acceptable, but product honesty still wins over punchy slogans.

## Recommended review checklist

Before shipping new marketing copy, confirm:

1. Is the tone right for the locale persona?
2. Is every product claim true in the checked-in repo?
3. Would the CTA feel natural to a landlord in that market?
4. Does the German version remain formal and legally credible?
5. Does the Polish version feel efficient and concrete rather than over-stylized?
6. Does the English version stay sharp without becoming vague or gimmicky?
