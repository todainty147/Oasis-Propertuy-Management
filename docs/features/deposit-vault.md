# Deposit Vault

Deposit Vault adds landlord-controlled deposit deduction records and settlement statement preparation to the existing finance and evidence workflows.

## Feature Flags

- `deposit_deductions_log`
- `deposit_settlement_statement`

Minimum plan: Growth. Pro and Operator/Agency inherit access. Starter accounts should not receive Deposit Vault unless a deliberate account-level feature flag override is enabled for a controlled support case.

## Routes

- `/finance/deposit-vault`

## Tables

- `deposit_settlements`
- `deposit_deductions`
- `deposit_deduction_evidence_links`
- `deposit_settlement_exports`
- `deposit_settlement_audit_events`

## What It Does

- Creates a Deposit Deductions Log for itemised landlord review.
- Builds a Deposit Settlement Statement with proposed deductions, evidence references and amount to return.
- Links deductions to existing inspection, document, maintenance and dispute-pack evidence.
- Preserves audit events for create, update, evidence link, statement generation, lock and archive actions.

## Field Reference

### Create Settlement

- `Property`: Required property for the settlement. This controls the property label shown in the settlement list and statement preview.
- `Tenant`: Optional tenant linked to the settlement. Tenant access is only relevant once a settlement is explicitly shared in a future workflow.
- `Deposit held amount`: Amount of deposit held for the tenancy. This is a record-keeping amount only; Tenaqo does not hold or move the money.
- `Currency`: Currency code used for display and statement calculations. The current default is `GBP`.
- `Jurisdiction`: High-level jurisdiction label, currently `UK`, `Poland` or `Other`.
- `Summary`: Free-text landlord summary for the proposed settlement.

### Settlement List

- `Property`: Property address from the linked property record.
- `Tenant`: Tenant name or email from the linked tenant record, or `No tenant linked`.
- `Deposit held`: Deposit amount entered on the settlement.
- `Deductions`: Sum of all deduction amounts linked to the settlement.
- `Return`: Deposit held minus deductions. Negative values indicate deductions exceed the recorded deposit.
- `Status`: Settlement lifecycle value, such as `draft`, `statement_generated`, `locked` or `archived`.
- `Evidence`: Shows whether any deductions are missing evidence links.

### Settlement Detail

- `Deposit held`: Recorded deposit amount.
- `Deductions total`: Total of all deduction row amounts.
- `Proposed return`: Deposit held minus deductions total.
- `Evidence attached`: Count of deductions with attached evidence.
- `Missing evidence`: Count of deductions still missing evidence.
- `Negative return warning`: Appears when deductions exceed the deposit held and the settlement should be reviewed before sharing.

### Settlement Actions

- `Generate statement`: Creates a statement export record and marks the settlement as `statement_generated`.
- `Create dispute pack`: Opens the Deposit Dispute Pack workflow so the landlord can prepare wider evidence.
- `Lock settlement`: Locks the settlement so deductions can no longer be edited in normal flows.
- `Archive`: Marks the settlement archived.

Locked and archived settlements are read-only in the page. The service layer also blocks deduction and evidence edits for locked settlements.

### Add Deduction

- `Deduction type`: Category for the proposed deduction. Current values are `cleaning`, `damage`, `missing_keys`, `rent_arrears`, `gardening`, `rubbish_removal`, `unpaid_bills`, `repair_invoice`, `replacement_item` and `other`.
- `Title`: Short landlord-facing label for the deduction.
- `Amount`: Proposed deduction amount.
- `Explanation`: Free-text explanation for why the deduction is being proposed.

### Deduction Schedule

- `Title`: Deduction title.
- `Deduction type`: Category selected when the deduction was created.
- `Evidence status`: Current evidence state: `missing`, `partial`, `attached` or `needs_review`.
- `Amount`: Proposed deduction amount.
- `Description`: Deduction explanation.

### Evidence Links

- `Select deduction`: Deduction row that the evidence should support.
- `Evidence type`: Type of supporting evidence. Current values include Evidence Vault report/item, inspection photo, maintenance request, work order, invoice, quote, receipt, tenancy agreement, communication, note and other.
- `Evidence label or reference`: Human-readable evidence label or reference id.
- `Notes`: Free-text context explaining why the evidence supports the deduction.

### Statement Preview

- `Title`: Statement title generated by Tenaqo.
- `Property`: Linked property address or fallback property id.
- `Tenant`: Linked tenant name/email or fallback tenant id.
- `Evidence index`: Public-safe evidence list with deduction number, evidence type, label and notes. Internal account ids, link ids, deduction ids and document ids are not exposed in statement metadata.
- `Disclaimer`: Safe wording explaining that the statement is an organisational record, does not guarantee dispute outcomes, and does not replace legal advice.

## Settlement Statement Disclaimer

Deposit Settlement Statements are organisational records for landlord review. They do not replace legal advice, do not guarantee the outcome of any deposit dispute, and do not mean Tenaqo holds funds, moves money or operates as a payment rail.

## Maintenance Smart Diagnostics Link

Maintenance Smart Diagnostics can flag possible deposit evidence for landlord review. It does not decide tenant liability or create deductions automatically.

When enabled with `maintenance_deposit_evidence_linking`, any maintenance diagnostic handoff must keep the deduction amount blank or landlord-entered, label the context as possible deposit evidence, and write the usual Deposit Vault audit events only after landlord confirmation.

## Access Model

Owners/admins/staff who can manage the account can manage settlement records. Tenants can read only records explicitly shared for their tenant profile. Contractors do not receive access to the settlement or deduction log.

Structured evidence references are checked against the referenced record's `account_id` for known Evidence Vault, maintenance, work-order and document evidence types. Manual `note` and `other` links do not require a structured evidence id.

## Limitations

- Browser print/export remains the current PDF pattern.
- Tenaqo does not collect rent, hold deposits, move money or operate as a payment rail.
- The workflow supports dispute preparation and landlord review only.
